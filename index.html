Sentence Structure 🏛️

Unlike English (Subject-Verb-Object), Japanese follows an SOV (Subject-Object-Verb) blueprint. The verb must always sit at the end of the sentence.

Structure Example:

English: I eat an apple.
Japanese Blueprint: Subject は Object を Verb。
In Action: 私 は りんご を 食べます。
(Watashi wa ringo wo tabemasu)

Verb Conjugation (Formal) ⚙️

Group 1: U-Verbs (Drop -u, add -i base)

Drop final dictionary vowel sound and swap to 'i' variant before attaching endings.
• Iku (Go): Ik + masu = Ikimasu

Group 2: Ru-Verbs (Drop -ru)

Simply remove the final ru and add the suffix.
• Taberu (Eat): Tabe + masu = Tabemasu
FormEndingExample (Iku)
Pres. Positive+masuIkimasu (Go)
Pres. Negative+masenIkimasen (Don't Go)
Past Positive+mashitaIkimashita (Went)
Past Negative+masen deshitaIkimasen deshita

Adjective Conjugation 🎨

I-Adjectives (Drop final -i)

Drop final -i character before joining negative and past variations.
• Oishii: Oishi + kunaidesu = Oishikunaidesu

Na-Adjectives (Keep dictionary word)

Treat base dictionary entry as a noun; append variations directly onto it.
• Kirei: Kirei + deshita = Kireideshita

Particles Masterlist & Critical Nuances 🗺️

Particle Key Roles & Critical Nuances Contextual Examples
は (wa)
  • Topic Marker: Identifies the main theme of the sentence.
  • Contrast: Emphasizes differences between items or elements.
 Kyou wa samui desu.
(As for today, it is cold.)

Ocha wa nomimasu ga, kohi wa nomimasen.
(I drink green tea, but NOT coffee.)
が (ga)
  • Subject Marker: Marks grammatical subject explicitly.
  • New Info: Introduces unpredictable, raw facts or natural events.
  • Desire/Ability: Automatically accompanies tags like suki, hoshii, wakariru.
 Ame ga furimasu.
(Rain falls.)

Watashi wa ringo ga suki desu.
(I like apples.)
を (wo)
  • Direct Object: Direct target recipient of intentional active verbs.
  • Motion Space: Identifies an area through which something travels or transits.
 Hon コンを yomimasu.
(I read books.)

Koen を sanpo shimasu.
(Walk through the park.)
に (ni)
  • Static Location: Marks placement existence (with iru/aru).
  • Specific Time: Fixed numerical instances (hours, months).
  • Destination Target: Landing terminal point for directional actions.
 Nihon に imasu.
(I am in Japan.)

Gokkou に ikimasu.
(Go to school.)
で (de)
  • Active Location: Where an interactive movement occurs.
  • Tool / Means: Material, tool, vehicle, or option utilized.
 Uchi で tabemasu.
(Eat at home.)

Basu で ikimasu.
(Go by bus.)
へ (he)
  • Direction: Highlights the heading path vector toward a space (pronounced 'e').
 Kyoto へ mukaimasu.
(Head toward Kyoto.)
と (to)
  • Exhaustive Linker: Explicit complete listing connection ("and").
  • Companion: Together along with another individual.
 Pen と kami.
(A pen and paper.)

Tomodachi と aimashita.
(Met with a friend.)
の (no)
  • Possession / Modifier: Connects nouns together directly.
 Anata の kuruma.
(Your car.)
も (mo)
  • Inclusion Parallel: Translates to "also", "too", or "neither".
 Kare も sensei desu.
(He is also a teacher.)
から (kara)
  • Starting Bounds: The fundamental source origin point ("from").
  • Reason: Placed at end of clauses meaning "because".
 Hachiji から benkyou shimasu.
(Study from 8:00.)
まで (made)
  • Ending Bounds: Ultimate destination or time limit ("until").
 Shibuya まで arukimasu.
(Walk up to Shibuya.)
か (ka)
  • Question Ending: Serves as an audible punctuation mark.
  • Choice Connector: Means "or" when dividing nouns.
 Kore desu か？
(Is it this one?)
ね (ne)
  • Agreement Tag: Seeking affirmation ("isn't it?").
 Ii tenki desu ね.
(Beautiful weather, right?)
よ (yo)
  • Certainty Tag: Informing new details with strong conviction ("I tell you!").
 Kore wa oishii desu よ!
(This is delicious, I tell you!)
より (yori)
  • Comparison Base: Sets the baseline reference point ("than").
 Kuruha より densha ga hayai.
(Trains are faster than cars.)
ほど (hodo)
  • Relative Extent: Sets measurement constraints ("to the degree of").
 Kesa wa kyou ほど samukunai.
(This morning wasn't as cold as today.)
しか (shika)
  • Exclusive Bound: Means "nothing but" (Must pair with negative verb forms).
 Mizu しか arimasen.
(There is nothing but water.)
だけ (dake)
  • Limit Bound: Means "only / just" (Pairs with positive verb forms).
 Ringo だけ tabemashita.
(I only ate an apple.)
など (nado)
  • Non-Exhaustive: Implies more items exist unlisted ("and things like...").
 Hon ya pen など を kaimashita.
(I bought books, pens, and such things.)
