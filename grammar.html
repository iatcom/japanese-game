Sentence Structure 🏛️

Unlike English (Subject-Verb-Object), Japanese follows an SOV (Subject-Object-Verb) blueprint. The verb must always sit at the end of the sentence.

Structure Example:

English: I eat an apple.
Japanese Blueprint: Subject は Object を Verb。
In Action: 私 は りんご を 食べます。
(Watashi wa ringo wo tabemasu)

Verb Conjugation (Formal) ⚙️

Group 1: U-Verbs (Drop -u, add -i base)

Drop final dictionary vowel sound and swap to 'i' variant before attaching endings.
• Iku (Go): Ik + masu = Ikimasu

Group 2: Ru-Verbs (Drop -ru)

Simply remove the final ru and add the suffix.
• Taberu (Eat): Tabe + masu = Tabemasu
FormEndingExample (Iku)
Pres. Positive+masuIkimasu (Go)
Pres. Negative+masenIkimasen (Don't Go)
Past Positive+mashitaIkimashita (Went)
Past Negative+masen deshitaIkimasen deshita

Adjective Conjugation 🎨

I-Adjectives (Drop final -i)

Drop final -i character before joining negative and past variations.
• Oishii: Oishi + kunaidesu = Oishikunaidesu

Na-Adjectives (Keep dictionary word)

Treat base dictionary entry as a noun; append variations directly onto it.
• Shizuka (Quiet): Shizuka + deshita = Shizukadeshita (Note: Exceptions like Kirei look like i-adj but conjugate as na-adj!)

Particles Masterlist & Critical Nuances 🗺️

Particle Key Roles & Nuances Examples
は (wa) Topic: Main theme.
Contrast: Stresses difference. 		Kyou wa samui desu.
(Today is cold.)

Ocha wa nomimasu.
(I drink tea [specifically].)
が (ga) Subject: Acts out verb.
State: Abilities / desires (suki).
But: Conjunction clause. 		Ame ga furimasu.
(Rain falls.)

Ringo ga suki desu.
(I like apples.)
を (wo) Direct Object: Receives action.
Path: Area of movement. 		Hon を yomimasu.
(Read books.)

Kouen を sanpo shimasu.
(Walk through park.)
に (ni) Static Location: (iru/aru).
Specific Time: Clock/calendar.
Destination: Landing point. 		Nihon に imasu.
(In Japan.)

Shichiji に okimasu.
(Wake up at 7.)

Gakkou に ikimasu.
(Go to school.)
で (de) Active Location: Action spot.
Tool/Means: Resource/vehicle. 		Uchi で tabemasu.
(Eat at home.)

Basu で ikimasu.
(Go by bus.)
へ (he) Directional: Trajectory towards. Kyoto へ mukaimasu.
(Heading toward Kyoto.)
と (to) And: Exhaustive noun list.
With: Companion.

(Pen and paper.)

Tomodachi と hanashimashita.
(Talked with friend.)
の (no) Possession: Ownership/modifier. Anata の kuruma.
(Your car.)

Classifiers & Counters (助数詞) 🔢

In Japanese, items are counted by attaching a specific counter suffix to the number.

General Placement Rule:

Item + Particle + [ Quantity + Counter ] + Verb
• Kouhihi wo [ ip-pai ] kudasai. (One cup of coffee, please.)
• Hon ga [ ni-satsu ] arimasu. (There are two books.)
Counter Usage / Category 1st & 2nd Items Example
〜つ (Tsu) General objects (1-10) 1: Hitotsu (ひとつ)
2: Futatsu (ふたつ)
〜人 (Nin) People 1: Hitori (一人)
2: Futari (二人)
〜本 (Hon) Long, cylindrical items (pens, bottles, trees) 1: Ippon (一本)
2: Nihon (二本)
〜枚 (Mai) Flat, thin objects (paper, shirts, plates) 1: Ichimai (一枚)
2: Nimai (二枚)
〜冊 (Satsu) Bound items (books, notebooks) 1: Issatsu (一冊)
2: Nisatsu (二冊)
〜台 (Dai) Machines, vehicles, appliances 1: Ichidai (一台)
2: Nidai (二台)
〜個 (Ko) Small, round, or compact objects 1: Ikko (一個)
2: Niko (二個)
〜匹 (Hiki) Small animals, fish, insects 1: Ippiki (一匹)
2: Nihiki (二匹)
〜杯 (Hai) Cups, glasses, bowls of drink/food 1: Ippai (一杯)
2: Nihai (二杯)
